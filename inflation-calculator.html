Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average US inflation rate? ▼
Historical US average: ~3%/year. Fed targets 2%. In 2022 it peaked near 9%. Long-term planning: use 2.5-3%.
How does inflation affect savings? ▼
If savings earn 1% but inflation is 3%, you lose 2% real purchasing power annually. Investments must beat inflation.
What beats inflation? ▼
Historically: stocks (7-10%/yr), real estate, I-bonds (inflation-adjusted), TIPS. Cash savings typically lose to inflation.
