Investment 101 – Building Real Wealth

Investing is putting money to work so it grows over time. Unlike saving in a bank, investing carries risk — but historically, long-term investing in diversified assets has been the #1 way ordinary people build wealth.

Key Investment Metrics Explained

  • ROI (Return on Investment): (Profit ÷ Cost) × 100 = percentage gain
  • CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate): Average annual return accounting for compounding
  • Dividend Yield: Annual dividend ÷ stock price × 100
  • P/E Ratio: Price ÷ Earnings per share — valuation metric

💰 The Simplest Investing Strategy

Invest in a low-cost S&P 500 index fund every month regardless of market conditions (dollar-cost averaging). Keep expense ratio under 0.1%. Reinvest dividends. Don't sell during downturns. This has beaten 90%+ of professional fund managers historically.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ROI for investment? ▼
Stocks (S&P 500): ~10% annual historically. Real estate: 7-12% including rental income. Bonds: 3-5%. Savings: 4-5% current high-yield. Anything above inflation (3%) is growing real wealth.
How much should I invest per month? ▼
Rule of thumb: save and invest 15-20% of gross income. Starting salary $50,000: invest $625-833/month. Even $100/month started at 25 becomes $350,000 by 65 at 8% return.
What is the difference between trading and investing? ▼
Trading = frequent buying/selling for short-term gains (risky, time-consuming, most traders lose money). Investing = buying and holding for years/decades (proven wealth builder for most people). Most experts recommend long-term investing over trading.
Should I invest or pay off debt first? ▼
High-interest debt (credit cards, personal loans above 8%): pay off first. Low-interest debt (mortgage, student loans below 6%): invest and pay debt simultaneously. Always contribute enough to get full employer 401k match — that's 50-100% instant return.
How to start investing with little money? ▼
Most brokers have $0 minimums now. Start with index ETFs (Vanguard VTI, Fidelity ZERO funds). Automate monthly contributions. Start with whatever you can — even $25/month builds the habit and compounds over time.

People Also Ask

What's the safest investment with good returns? ▼
I-bonds (inflation-adjusted, US government), FDIC-insured high-yield savings accounts, short-term Treasury bills. For slightly more return with low risk: total market index funds held for 10+ year timeframe.
How long does it take to double your money? ▼
Rule of 72: divide 72 by return rate. At 7%: 10.3 years. At 8%: 9 years. At 10%: 7.2 years. At 6%: 12 years.
