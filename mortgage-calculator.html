💡 Did You Know?

On a typical 30-year mortgage, you pay more in interest than the actual price of your home. That means the bank technically makes more money from your home than you do in the first 15 years.

How Does a Mortgage Calculator Work?

A mortgage calculator uses a simple but powerful formula to figure out how much you owe each month. The math behind it is called amortization — a fancy word for "spreading out your payments over time."

The Formula (Don't Worry, We Do the Math)

Monthly Payment = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]

Where: P = loan amount, r = monthly interest rate (annual ÷ 12), n = total payments (years × 12)

💡 Most People Don't Realize This

In the first year of a 30-year mortgage, nearly 80% of your payment goes to interest — not the actual loan. You're barely reducing what you owe.

Why the Total Cost is So High

Interest compounds every month. Even a 1% difference in your rate can mean $50,000–$100,000 more over 30 years. This is why getting the best rate — and making even small extra payments — matters enormously.

💰 Money-Saving Tip

Paying just $200 extra per month on a $300,000 mortgage at 7% saves over $98,000 in interest and cuts 6 years off your loan. Use the "Extra Payment" tab above to see your savings.

Principal vs Interest — Where Does Your Money Go?

Every payment is split between two things: principal (reducing your debt) and interest (paying the bank). Early on, the split is brutal — most goes to interest. Over time, the balance shifts in your favor.

Common Mortgage Mistakes That Cost You Thousands

  1. Not shopping around for rates — Even 0.25% difference = $15,000+ over 30 years
  2. Choosing the longest term always — Lower monthly payment, but you pay 2x more total
  3. Skipping extra payments — $100/month extra = $50,000+ saved over time
  4. Ignoring PMI — Private Mortgage Insurance adds $100–300/month if down payment under 20%
  5. Not factoring in property taxes and insurance — These can add 20–30% to your real monthly cost

How to Get a Lower Mortgage Rate

  • Improve your credit score above 740 for best rates
  • Make a larger down payment (20%+ avoids PMI)
  • Compare at least 3–5 lenders before committing
  • Consider paying "points" upfront to lower your rate
  • Choose a shorter term if you can afford higher payments

Real Questions People Ask About Mortgages

These are actual questions people search for on Google, Reddit, and Quora — answered simply:

How much mortgage can I afford on a $80,000 salary? ▼
Using the 28% rule: 28% of $80,000 = $22,400/year = $1,867/month for housing. At 7% for 30 years, this supports roughly a $280,000 mortgage. Add your down payment to find max home price. Always include property tax and insurance in your calculation.
What happens if I pay an extra $500 a month on my mortgage? ▼
On a $300,000 mortgage at 7%, paying $500 extra monthly saves approximately $175,000 in interest and pays off your mortgage 11 years early. Use our Extra Payment tab above to calculate your specific savings.
Is it better to pay off mortgage or invest? ▼
If your mortgage rate is above 6-7%, paying it off early gives guaranteed return equal to your rate. If below 6%, historically investing in index funds has returned more (7-10%/year). Most financial experts suggest doing both — make extra mortgage payments AND invest.
How much is a $400,000 mortgage per month? ▼
At 7% interest for 30 years, a $400,000 mortgage costs approximately $2,661/month (principal + interest only). Add property tax (~$400-600/month), homeowner's insurance (~$150/month), and possibly PMI. Total monthly cost: ~$3,200-3,400+.
What credit score do I need for a mortgage in 2026? ▼
Conventional loans: 620 minimum, but 740+ gets the best rates. FHA loans: 580 with 3.5% down. VA loans: no minimum but lenders prefer 620+. Each 20-point improvement in score can save $50-100/month on a typical mortgage.
How do UK mortgage rates compare to USA in 2026? ▼
UK mortgage rates in 2026 are typically 4.5-6% for 2-5 year fixed products. US 30-year fixed rates are around 6.5-7.5%. UK mortgages are usually shorter terms (2-5 year fixed, then renegotiated). US has the unique 30-year fixed mortgage product not found elsewhere.
How much deposit do I need to buy a house in Australia? ▼
In Australia, standard deposit is 20% of property value to avoid Lender's Mortgage Insurance (LMI). For a $700,000 Sydney property, that's $140,000. First home buyers can access government schemes allowing 5% deposits with LMI coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions – Mortgage Calculator

How is monthly mortgage payment calculated? ▼
Monthly Payment = Loan Amount × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n-1], where r = annual rate ÷ 12 and n = years × 12. Example: $300,000 at 7% for 30 years = $1,996/month. Our calculator handles this instantly — just enter your numbers above.
What is the 28/36 rule for mortgages? ▼
Lenders use this guideline: housing costs (mortgage + tax + insurance) should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income. Total debt payments (housing + car + loans) should not exceed 36%. Going above these increases risk of rejection and financial strain.
What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval? ▼
Pre-qualification is a quick estimate based on self-reported info — not verified by the bank. Pre-approval involves actual credit check and income verification. Sellers take pre-approval more seriously. Always get pre-approved before making offers.
How does refinancing a mortgage work? ▼
Refinancing replaces your current mortgage with a new one — usually at a lower rate. You'll pay closing costs ($3,000-10,000 typically). Break-even point = closing costs ÷ monthly savings. If you plan to stay past break-even, refinancing usually makes sense. Use our Refinance Calculator.
What is PMI and how can I avoid it? ▼
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required when your down payment is under 20%. It costs 0.5-1.5% of loan annually (~$100-300/month on a $200,000 loan). Avoid it by: 20%+ down payment, piggyback loan (80/10/10), or lender-paid PMI at slightly higher rate.
Should I get a fixed or variable (adjustable) rate mortgage? ▼
Fixed rate: same payment for entire term — best if staying long-term or rates are rising. ARM (Adjustable Rate): lower initial rate that adjusts after 3-7 years — good if selling before adjustment period or rates are expected to fall. In high-rate environments, many experts favor fixed.
How much does 1% difference in mortgage rate cost over 30 years? ▼
On a $300,000 mortgage: 6% = $1,799/month ($347,514 total interest) | 7% = $1,996/month ($418,527 total interest). The 1% difference costs $71,000 more over 30 years. This is why shopping for the best rate is one of the most important financial decisions you'll make.
How does mortgage work in Canada vs USA? ▼
Key differences: Canada has maximum 25-year amortization for insured mortgages (vs USA's 30-year). Canada rates are renewed every 1-5 years (not locked for full term). Canada requires mortgage insurance on less than 20% down regardless of lender. Both use similar amortization math.
What happens if I miss a mortgage payment? ▼
Day 1-15: Grace period, no penalty. Day 15-30: Late fee charged. Day 30+: Credit score impact begins. Day 90-120: Pre-foreclosure process may start. Always contact your lender before missing a payment — they have hardship programs and forbearance options.
Is rent vs buying a home better financially? ▼
No universal answer. Buying builds equity and locks in payment, but has high upfront costs and maintenance. Renting provides flexibility and no maintenance, but builds no equity. General rule: if you plan to stay 5+ years and can afford 20% down, buying often wins. Under 5 years, renting is usually smarter.

Can I pay off a 30-year mortgage in 15 years? ▼

What is mortgage amortization? ▼
Amortization is the process of paying off your mortgage in regular installments over time. Each payment covers both interest and principal. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal. An amortization schedule shows every payment breakdown.
How much is a $200,000 mortgage per month? ▼
