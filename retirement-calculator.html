How Much Do You Really Need to Retire?

The most widely used rule: save 25 times your annual expenses. At a 4% withdrawal rate, your savings should theoretically last 30+ years. If you spend $50,000/year, you need $1,250,000 saved.

The 4% Withdrawal Rule Explained

Research (the "Trinity Study") shows that withdrawing 4% of your portfolio annually has historically lasted 30+ years in most market conditions. Withdraw $50,000/year → need $1,250,000. But some experts now suggest 3-3.5% for longer retirements or early retirees.

💡 Retirement Reality Check

Average American retires with less than $300,000 saved — far below the $1M+ recommended. But starting at 25 with $300/month at 7% return reaches $1 million by age 65. The math works — but you must start.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do I need to retire at 60? ▼
At 60 with 25 years of retirement and $50,000/year expenses: need $1.25M. Social Security at 60 isn't available (USA minimum 62). Factor in healthcare costs — major expense before Medicare at 65.
Can I retire at 55 with $1 million? ▼
Depends on expenses. At 4% withdrawal: $40,000/year. If expenses are under $40,000 and you have Social Security/pension later, possibly. Consider sequence-of-returns risk with early retirement.
What is the best retirement account? ▼
USA: 401k with employer match first, then Roth IRA (tax-free growth), then traditional IRA. UK: Workplace pension first, then SIPP. Canada: Employer pension first, then RRSP, then TFSA. Australia: Super always.
How does inflation affect retirement savings? ▼
At 3% inflation, $50,000 expenses today = $80,000 in 20 years. Your savings must grow faster than inflation. Use 4-5% real return (after inflation) for retirement projections rather than nominal rate.
Should I delay retirement to increase Social Security? ▼
Each year you delay Social Security (USA) past 62 increases benefit by 6-8%/year, up to 70. Delaying from 62 to 70 can increase monthly benefit by 70%+. Major financial decision — calculate break-even age.

People Also Ask

What is the average retirement savings by age? ▼
USA averages: Age 35: $37,000. Age 45: $87,000. Age 55: $185,000. Age 65: $232,000. These averages are dragged up by high earners — medians are much lower. Don't compare; focus on your own 25x goal.
How much Social Security will I get? ▼
Depends on earning history. Maximum 2026 benefit at full retirement age: ~$3,900/month. Average: ~$1,900/month. Check ssa.gov for your personalized estimate.
